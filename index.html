Titan Arum

Believed to be one of the largest and rarest flowers, the corpse flower is found in Indonesian rainforests and can reach up to 12 feet (3.6 meters) in its natural habitat. It is known as the corpse flower because it exudes an odor similar to that of rotten meat when it blooms.

Flame Lily

Native to Asia and tropical and southern Africa, these beautiful and unique flowers are also regarded as the national flower of Zimbabwe. These should be handled with care as parts of it are poisonous.
Know more...

Corpse Lily

This flower is also found in Indonesia. Said to be the largest in the world, it can grow up to 3 feet (one meter) in diameter. It produces an almost identical odor to that of the Titan Arum when in bloom. It has no visible stem, roots or leaves.
Know more...

Lady's Slipper Orchid

This member of the orchid family is shaped like a lady’s slipper and, within the U.K., is protected by the Wildlife and Countryside Act of 1981. It is also found in Asia, some European nations including Spain, among other regions.
Know more...

Jade Vine

Restricted to the rainforests of the Philippines, this beautiful claw-shaped flower is in danger of going extinct in the wild because its natural habitat is being destroyed at an alarming rate. The flowers vary in color from blue to light green. Their natural pollinators are bats.
Know more...

Ghost Orchid

The ghost orchid, named so because of the shape of its petals, is found in areas of Cuba, Florida and the Bahamas. The plant is rare because it is almost impossible to propagate. It has no leaves, does not depend on photosynthesis and does not manufacture its own food. It also requires specific host trees to grow and perfect conditions for it to flower. It is considered an endangered plant in Florida.
Know more...

Chocolate Cosmos

Native to Mexico, this flower has reportedly been extinct in the wild for over 100 years. The flowers have a rich deep-brown color and emit, as the name suggests, a rich chocolate fragrance. Today, the clones of these flowers exist in protected areas.
Know more...

Gibraltar Campion

Endemic to Gibraltar, the flower was thought to be extinct in the wild. However, in 1994 they were found in the British Overseas Territory’s Upper Rock Nature Preserve. Since then, a few specimens have been artificially grown at the Kew Royal Botanic Gardens in London, England. The flowers range in color from violet to bright pink.
Know more...

Middlemist's Red

The Middlemist’s Red camellia is considered the rarest flower in the world. Only two known examples are believed to exist, one in New Zealand and another one in England. The plant was brought from China to England in 1804 by John Middlemist.

Franklin Tree Flower

The Franklin tree produces beautiful white flowers with clusters of golden yellow stamens in the center. Believed to be native to an area near Fort Barrington along the Altamaha River in Georgia, U.S., it has been extinct in the wild since the early 1800s.
